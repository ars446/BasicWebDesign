Therapeutic Garden Opens at Kyiv's Expocenter

New Green Space Supports Veterans' Recovery

A therapeutic garden was officially opened in October 2025 at the Expocenter of Ukraine (VDNG) in Kyiv. The garden was designed to support the physical and psychological recovery of veterans and civilians affected by the ongoing conflict.

The project combines nature-based healing with adaptive outdoor activities. Visitors can walk through sensory paths, tend raised garden beds, and participate in guided relaxation sessions surrounded by greenery. Specialists say that interaction with nature can significantly reduce stress and improve mental well-being.

Gardens have a unique power to heal, said project coordinator Olena Marchenko. We wanted to create a space where people can reconnect with nature and find a moment of peace.

The therapeutic garden at VDNG represents a new approach to rehabilitation in Ukraine-one that uses the restorative power of nature to help those who have experienced trauma.
- Rubryka, October 2025

The garden features accessible pathways, seating areas, and horticultural therapy stations. It was created through collaboration between local nonprofits, landscape architects, and the Kyiv city administration.

The Expocenter of Ukraine, commonly known as VDNG, is one of the largest exhibition complexes in Europe. Located in the Holosiivskyi district, the complex has been gradually transforming into a modern public park and cultural hub.
